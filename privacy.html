Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026

Kin helps you and your person create phone-light Moments together. Kin is built around a simple stance: presence, not surveillance.

What Kin can see — and what it can't

  • Your app choices stay on your device. Kin uses Apple's Screen Time (Family Controls) technology. Apple's design means Kin's developers can never see which apps you choose to quiet, which apps you use, or how much you use them. The tokens Screen Time provides are opaque even to Kin.
  • Your partner never sees your app activity. Pause requests share only a duration and an optional note you write yourself. They never include an app name. There is no usage reporting, no screenshots, no browsing history — nothing to report, because Kin cannot see it either.
  • The shared history contains only Moment events: Moments started and completed, pause requests and their outcomes, emergency pauses, and pairing changes.

What Kin stores

  • On your device and in your iCloud: your display name, your partner link, your Moment schedules, your activity history, and your Screen Time selection (device-only). Shared state syncs between you and your partner through your personal iCloud (CloudKit). Kin does not operate its own servers for this data — there is no Kin account and no Kin database.
  • Notifications are used for pause requests, emergency-pause notices, and Moment reminders. You can decline them; Kin still works.

Anonymous usage statistics

To understand which features help and which don't, Kin counts anonymous events — for example "a Moment was started" or "onboarding was completed" — along with durations. These counts are tied to a random installation identifier, never to your name, email, or Apple ID, and they never include which apps you quiet, your notes, your custom Moment names, or anything about your partner. Processing is handled by TelemetryDeck, a privacy-focused, GDPR-compliant service that anonymizes data on arrival. Kin does not use this data for advertising and cannot use it to identify you.

What Kin does not do

  • No ads, no ad tracking, no sale of data.
  • No behavioral profiles — usage statistics are anonymous counts, never tied to who you are.
  • No location collection.
  • No reading of messages, photos, or any app content.

Children

Kin is designed for consenting adults — couples, co-parents, and accountability partners — each managing their own phone. Kin does not supervise children's devices.

Deleting your data

Disconnecting a partner removes the link on both sides. Deleting the app removes on-device data. Data synced through iCloud lives in your personal iCloud account and can be removed from your device's iCloud settings (Settings → your name → iCloud → Manage Account Storage). Because Kin has no accounts and no servers of its own, there is nothing else to delete.

Purchases

Kin Plus subscriptions and purchases are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. Kin never sees your payment details.
